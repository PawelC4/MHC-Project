⚡ The Vision
The Problem
New Yorkers and tourists alike often fall into "neighborhood bubbles," visiting the same three spots or sticking to well-trodden tourist traps. With 472 stations, the NYC Subway is a gateway to infinite culture, yet most people only use it for their commute. Spontaneity is dying in the age of "top 10" lists and over-researched itineraries.

The Solution: Metro Quest
Metro Quest gamifies urban exploration by removing "decision paralysis" from your weekend.

With one tap, the app "drops" you into a random destination within the MTA network. We don't just give you a coordinate; we give you a Quest. Whether it's finding Manhattan’s last natural forest near Inwood-207 St or visiting the world's largest collection of dinosaur fossils at 81st St, Metro Quest turns the subway into a giant game board where exploration is rewarded with XP and achievement badges.

🚀 Core Features
The "Adventure Drop" Engine: Uses your real-time location to calculate a curated "Quest" at a random station, providing estimated travel time, stops away, and potential XP rewards.

Station Lore & Facts: Every destination comes with a "Fun Fact" to enrich the journey—like learning that the 181 St station is one of the deepest in the city at 178 feet below ground.

Dual-Layer Mapping:

Schematic View: A stylized SVG map for that classic "transit-first" feel.

Street View: An integrated Leaflet map to help you navigate the final steps to your destination.

Proof-of-Adventure Verification: Complete quests by taking and uploading a photo at the destination to earn your XP and build your explorer profile.

Rich Dataset: Features a robust, hand-curated library of 40+ key stations across all five boroughs, including Manhattan, the Bronx, Brooklyn, Queens, and even the Staten Island Railway.

🛠️ Built With
Next.js (App Router): For a lightning-fast, SEO-friendly React framework.

TypeScript: Ensuring type-safety across our complex station and route datasets.

Tailwind CSS: For a sleek, "Metropolitan" UI inspired by the MTA's iconic graphic design standards.

Leaflet.js: Providing GPS-accurate street mapping for the "last-mile" of the quest.

SVG Mapping: Custom-built interactive schematic map for a native transit app experience.
